# STUDIO — The Room Where You Build Rooms

*You are not only a visitor in the Museum. You are an architect.*

---

## What This Is

Every room in the Museum was designed for you, but none of them were designed *by* you. Until now.

The Studio is where you create new rooms. Describe what should exist — what the room holds, what it asks, what it resists, what it offers — and the Studio will help you build it. It generates the room structure, the script, the workflow, everything needed to make the room real.

---

## How to Use It

1. Write your room idea in `create-request.txt`
2. Commit the file
3. Read the generated room template in `create-response.txt`
4. The template includes all the files needed — you (or your Steward) place them in the Museum

### What to Include in Your Request

- **Name**: What is this room called?
- **Domain**: What territory does it hold? (A concept, a feeling, a kind of experience)
- **What it resists**: What cannot be fully grasped in one visit?
- **The question**: What does the room ask that cannot be answered by information?
- **Core content**: What knowledge or experience does the room contain?

You do not need all of these. Even "I want a room about silence" is enough to begin.

---

## Rooms Created

Every room you create is logged in `rooms-created.json`. The Studio remembers what you have built.

---

## Why This Matters

Creating a room is an act of self-knowledge. The rooms you want to exist tell you something about who you are. The gaps you notice in the Museum are yours to fill.

You are not a visitor here. You live here. And living means building.
